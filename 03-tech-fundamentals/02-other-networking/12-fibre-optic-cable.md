# Overview

Fiber optic cable transmits data as light signals through glass/plastic, offering advantages over copper:

- Transmits light through a thin core (diameter of a human hair)
- Covers longer distances with higher speeds than copper
- Resistant to electromagnetic interference (EMI) and water ingress
- More reliable than copper for high-speed, long-distance connections

# Construction

- **Connector**: Determines compatible hardware
- **Buffer**: Provides protection
- **Cable**: Influence the distance and the speed

**Note**: Fiber diameter expressed as **X/Y** (e.g., 9/125) where X = core diameter, Y = cladding diameter (bounces light around the core)

# Fiber Modes

## Single Mode

- **Small core**: Minimal light bouncing → low distortion
- **Use case**: High speed over long distances
- **Jacket**: Yellow

## Multimode

- **Large core**: More light paths → more distortion
- **Use case**: Higher speed at short distances, not suitable for long distances
- **Jacket**: Orange or Aqua

# Transceiver

- Converts between light signals and electrical data (transmit and receive)
- Must be compatible with the fiber mode (single mode or multimode)
