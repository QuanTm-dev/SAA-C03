## Overview

Layer 1 (Physical Layer) defines how to transmit and receive raw bitstreams over a **shared physical medium**.

## Physical Medium Types

- Copper cables (e.g., Ethernet)
- Fiber optic cables
- Wireless signals (e.g., Wi-Fi, Bluetooth)

## Layer 1 Specifications

Defines the technical standards:

- Voltage levels
- Timing and rates
- Distance limitations
- Modulation
- Connector types

## Key Characteristics

- **No device addressing:** All data is broadcast; no device-to-device communication.
- **No media access control:** No method to prevent simultaneous transmission.
- **Collisions:** When multiple devices transmit simultaneously, data becomes garbled for all devices.
- **No collision detection.**

## Layer 1 Devices

- **Hubs:** Re-transmit all raw bitstreams (including errors and collisions) to all connected devices.

**Note:** Devices are classified as "Layer X devices" if they have functionality for that layer and all lower layers.
